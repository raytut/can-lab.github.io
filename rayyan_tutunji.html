Rayyan Tutunji

PhD student

I am a PhD candidate who mostly works with daily life stress reactivity and the brain. My studies examine differences in stress reactivity in the brain using fMRI, and will try to correlate these findings with stress reactivity in daily life using a mixture of ecological momentary assessments, and wearables that track physiology.

My previous work had a wide range of focus. During my masters I was part of a team looking into stress and dopamine in psychosis. I later worked at the American University of Beirut on studies regarding childhood development.
